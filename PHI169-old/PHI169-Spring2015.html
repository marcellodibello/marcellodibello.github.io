Part 1 - Michael Brown

W 1

  • Wed January 28

    We will begin with a few remarks about the nature of reasoning and the distinction between premises and conclusions.
  • Euclid's Elements ( definitions , postulates and common notions )
  • Euclid's Elements (proofs of proposition I , II-III and IV-V)
  • Introduction (Wed slides)

W 2

  • Mon February 2 & Wed February 4

    We will examine how some of the media reported the shooting of Michael Brown.
  • Fox News, The Truth about Ferguson (video)
  • Fox News on MB and some missing statistics (slides)
  • Review on the notion of relevant premise (slides)

W 3

  • Mon February 9 & Wed February 11

    We will read the grand jury transcripts and the testimonies, among others, by officer Wilson and Dorian Johnson. (NB: St Louis Radio published the grand jury documents, with notes, here ; the NY Times also pulished the documents, with photographs, here )
  • Johnson's Testimony (vol 4)
  • Wilson's Testimony (vol 5)
  • Excerpts from Wilson's and Johnson's testimonies (slides)

W 4

  • Mon February 16 & Wed February 18

    There is no class on Feb 16; on Feb 18 we will continue with last week’s topic.
  • Did MB Charge? (WP article)
  • Summary of the testimonies (PBS)
  • Visual reconstructions (WP, independent)

W 5

  • Mon February 23 & Wed February 25

    We will examine the physical evidence, such as medical examinations and DNA evidence, about the shooting.
  • Relevant transcripts (vol 19, vol 20)
  • DNA evidence (report)
  • Autopsy (official, dept. of defense, MB's family)
  • slides about the physical evidence (location, DNA, medical examination, toxicology)
  • eyewitness and physical evidence (summary NB: source not identified!)

W 6

  • Mon March 2 & Wed March 4

    We will examine the assistant prosecutors’ role in the grand jury proceedings and their instructions to the grand jurors.
  • Grand jury instructions ( Vol 24 , MSNBC Video)
  • What a grand jury does (fact sheet)

Part 2 - Capitalism

W 7

  • Mon March 9 & Wed March 11

    A recent book by French economist Thomas Piketty sparked controversy on the question, how did the distribution of wealth change during the last one hundred years in the US and Europe?
  • Capitalism: basics (slides)
  • Piketty's book "Capital in the 21st Century" (TED talk , TED slides)
  • Debating r>g (Ray's criticism , Piketty's reply)
  • Questioning the data (FT's criticism , Piketty's reply)

W 8

  • Mon March 16 & Wed March 18

    The question for debate this week is, what's wrong with the 1%?
  • Defending the 1% (Mankiw , WSJ)
  • Replies to Mankiw's argument (Pollack , Krugman)
  • Income data (NY Times , Economic Policy Inst , American Enterprise Inst)

W 9

  • Mon March 23 & Wed March 25

    This week we continue the discussion about economic inequalities, focusing on the distinction between descriptive, etiological, normative and policy questions (slides). See recent data on wealth inequalities in the US (charts).

W 10

  • Mon March 30 & Wed April 1

    Billioner Warren Buffet famously said that his earnings are taxed less than the earnings of his employees who make less than him. The question for debate this week is, are the rich taxed enough?
  • Arguments for higher taxation on the rich (here) and against (here)
  • Intelligence Square (video)
